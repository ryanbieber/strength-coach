# Strongman Methodologies

This folder collects methods worth studying for strongman programming.

## Core Picks

- Event specificity
  - Train the implement, movement pattern, and fatigue profile of the contest event as closely as possible.
  - Good when the meet demands carries, loads, presses, or medleys instead of barbell-only work.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/strongman-template/
- Implement matching
  - When the exact implement is unavailable, choose the closest match for loading position, grip, path, and fatigue cost.
  - Good when equipment is limited.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/strongman-template/
- Fatigue under event conditions
  - Use carries, loads, and medleys to train grip and bracing under fatigue.
  - Good when the athlete loses performance as the event goes on.
  - Source: https://store.reactivetrainingsystems.com/blogs/general-training/rts-for-strongman
- Contest prep and low novelty
  - Keep the implement and event order stable as the show approaches and cut accessory noise.
  - Good when the athlete needs peak specificity.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/strength-and-conditioning-programs/

## Also Worth Studying

- Auto-regulated event work
  - Use RPE or fatigue-based planning so event work stays productive without overshooting recovery.
  - Good when strongman events vary a lot in absolute load.
  - Source: https://store.reactivetrainingsystems.com/blogs/rts-basics/tagged/strongman
- Strongman as strength plus conditioning
  - Blend barbell strength, event work, and work capacity in one block.
  - Good when the athlete needs more general conditioning without losing event specificity.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/strongman-template/
