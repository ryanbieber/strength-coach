# Powerlifting Program Catalog

This folder holds external powerlifting programs worth studying when building or reviewing a powerlifting block.

These are reference points, not fixed scripts. Use them to understand progression, volume distribution, peaking, and autoregulation.

## Core Picks

- Stronger by Science, `Program Bundle`
  - Six full 21-week programs designed to adapt to schedule, preferences, and rate of progress.
  - Useful when you want a flexible, evidence-based powerlifting base with autoregulation.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/program-bundle/
- Stronger by Science, `28 One-Lift Programs`
  - A free library of one-lift routines for squat, bench, and deadlift.
  - Useful when you want a large menu of low-friction single-lift routines to study.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/newsletter-archived/
- Barbell Medicine, `Powerlifting II`
  - A structured powerlifting program centered on strength and conditioning.
  - Useful when you want a formal powerlifting block that still leaves room for work capacity.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/powerlifting-ii-template/

## Also Worth Studying

- Barbell Medicine, `Strength II`
  - A maximal-strength-focused template centered on the squat, bench press, deadlift, and overhead press.
  - Useful when you want a broader strength template that still behaves like a powerlifting-adjacent block.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/strength-ii-template/
- Barbell Medicine, `Peaking Template`
  - A short peaking block useful for meet prep logic and taper structure.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/peaking-template-3/
