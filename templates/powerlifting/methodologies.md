# Powerlifting Methodologies

This folder collects methods worth studying for powerlifting programming.

## Core Picks

- RPE-based autoregulation
  - Adjust load based on readiness while keeping the main lifts in place.
  - Good when training stress changes week to week and percentage-only planning is too rigid.
  - Source: https://aacedu.reactivetrainingsystems.com/
- High-frequency practice with volume matched
  - Increase lift frequency when more technique practice is useful, as long as volume is managed.
  - Good when the athlete benefits from more exposure to the competition lifts.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/high-frequency-training-for-a-bigger-total-research-on-highly-trained-norwegian-powerlifters/
- Bridge / off-season accumulation blocks
  - Use a block to build work capacity and broaden the base before a meet-specific cycle.
  - Good when the athlete needs preparation before peaking.
  - Source: https://www.jtsstrength.com/getting-the-most-from-off-season-powerlifting-training/
- Meet peaking with more specificity and less accessory noise
  - Reduce volume, keep the competition lifts central, and practice attempts or heavy singles.
  - Good when the target date is close.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/strength-and-conditioning-programs/

## Also Worth Studying

- Frequency as a programming variable
  - Use higher frequency when it improves technique and total work quality.
  - Good when the athlete needs more practice on squat, bench, and deadlift.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/training-frequency/
- Block review / feedback loops
  - Review training blocks and adjust the next phase based on what the athlete actually tolerated.
  - Good when you want the program to react to performance instead of only to the calendar.
  - Source: https://store.reactivetrainingsystems.com/blogs/advanced-concepts/using-block-reviews
