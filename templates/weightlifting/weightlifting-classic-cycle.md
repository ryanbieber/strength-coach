# Weightlifting Classic Cycle

## Gist
- A classic lift template centered on snatch and clean and jerk frequency.
- Built to preserve positions, speed, and receiving strength while steadily increasing specificity.

## Best Use
- Skill-driven blocks.
- Peaking into a meet or test.
- Athletes who need repeated technical exposures.

## Phase Bias
- Primary phase: SPP to Peak.
- Use it when the classic lifts are already the center and the athlete needs repeated competition practice.

## Weekly Shape
- Day 1: snatch + snatch variation + overhead squat or front squat
- Day 2: clean and jerk + front squat + pull
- Day 3: snatch emphasis + upper back work
- Day 4: clean and jerk emphasis + pulls + squat support

## What To Steal
- Frequent competition-lift practice.
- Pulls and squats that support the classic lifts.
- Variations that improve positions rather than create noise.

## Blend Notes
- Keep the classic lifts in the plan all the way through the peak.
- Trim variations when the meet gets close.
- This can start as a specific build block and finish as a peak when the variation count drops.
