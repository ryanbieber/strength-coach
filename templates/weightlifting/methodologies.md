# Weightlifting Methodologies

This folder collects methods worth studying for Olympic weightlifting programming.

## Core Picks

- Position-first training
  - Build the start position, pull positions, receiving positions, and squat recovery before chasing maximal load.
  - Good when technique is the limiter.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/article/49/The-Olympic-Lift-Starting-Position-Snatch-Clean/
- Frequent competition-lift exposure
  - Keep snatch and clean & jerk in the plan often enough to preserve timing and skill.
  - Good when the athlete needs skill reinforcement more than variation.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/train/
- Strength-focused classic lift phases
  - Use block phases to push squat, pull, and pressing strength while still keeping the classics present.
  - Good when the athlete needs more base strength before a peak.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/olympic-weightlifting-training-program/27/Basic-Strength-Cycle/
- Peak with heavy singles and competition mesocycles
  - Finish with a competition phase that includes heavy singles for testing or competition readiness.
  - Good when the meet is approaching.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/olympic-weightlifting-training-program/55/Greg-Everett-2014-American-Open-Cycle/
- Burgener methodology
  - Use clear positions, drills, and progressions to teach the lifts from the ground up.
  - Good when the athlete needs cleaner mechanics and a better foundation.
  - Source: https://burgenerstrength.com/burgener-weightlifting-course/
- USAW coach education lens
  - Use the sport’s official coaching framework to structure learning, cues, and competition readiness.
  - Good when the athlete is preparing for sanctioned competition or formal coaching development.
  - Source: https://www.usaweightlifting.org/elite-education

## Also Worth Studying

- Technique work at productive but submaximal loads
  - Use loads that are heavy enough to matter but not so heavy that positions fall apart.
  - Good when the athlete needs more technique practice.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/video/1457/Intensity-For-Weightlifting-Technique-Work/
- Position and receiving-position emphasis
  - Put extra work into the catch and recovery positions when those are the limiter.
  - Good when the athlete has strength but misses lifts in the bottom.
  - Source: https://www.catalystathletics.com/article/1696/What-a-Lovely-Bottom-The-Snatch-Clean-Receiving-Positions/
- Four-day structure with consistent progress
  - Train regularly with a stable weekly schedule so progress comes from execution, not chaos.
  - Good when the athlete needs reliable repetition of the classic lifts.
  - Source: https://burgenerstrength.com/programming/
