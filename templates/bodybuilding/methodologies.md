# Bodybuilding Methodologies

This folder collects methods worth studying for bodybuilding and hypertrophy programming.

## Core Picks

- Volume landmarks
  - Use maintenance, effective, and maximum recoverable volumes to decide how much work to add.
  - Good when hypertrophy is the main goal and recovery needs to stay controlled.
  - Source: https://renaissanceperiodization.com/training-volume-landmarks-muscle-growth/
- Split-based exercise prioritization
  - Organize training by muscle groups so lagging areas can get more work and priority.
  - Good when muscle-size development is the main limiter.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/hypertrophy/bodybuilding-template/
- Sustainable contest-prep / offseason management
  - Use phases that preserve muscle, manage fatigue, and keep long-term career progress in mind.
  - Good when the athlete wants repeatable growth rather than a one-off peak.
  - Source: https://www.3dmusclejourney.com/blog/the-default-diet

## Also Worth Studying

- Advanced hypertrophy methods
  - Add isolation work, supersets, and other higher-volume tools when they support the goal.
  - Good when the athlete can tolerate more volume and needs more local stimulus.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/hypertrophy/bodybuilding-ii-template-new/
- Body-part split logic
  - Use a body-part split when the athlete needs higher local volume and clearer recovery windows.
  - Good when a more traditional bodybuilding distribution fits the schedule.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/hypertrophy/bodybuilding-template-bundle/
