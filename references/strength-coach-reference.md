# Strength Coach Reference

## 1. Intake Checklist
- Sport or goal: powerlifting, strongman, weightlifting, bodybuilding, or general strength.
- Timeline: competition date, meet cycle length, or off-season window.
- Constraints: injuries, tolerance, training age, schedule, equipment.
- Current limiter: speed, position, recovery, strength, skill, or body composition.

## 2. Pattern First
Choose the pattern before choosing the exact lift.

- Squat: back squat, front squat, safety bar squat, hack squat, split squat.
- Bench / press: competition bench, incline press, floor press, log press, axle press.
- Hinge: deadlift, trap-bar deadlift, Romanian deadlift, good morning.
- Weightlifting: snatch, clean and jerk, pulls, front squat, overhead receiving.
- Carry / load: farmers walk, yoke walk, sandbag carry, frame carry, stone load.
- Bodybuilding: press, pull, quad, hamstring, arm, and deltoid isolation families.

## 3. Common Substitutions
Preserve the pattern and target, then adjust the implement.

- No rack for squat: front squat, goblet squat, split squat, belt squat, hack squat.
- No barbell bench setup: dumbbell press, machine press, floor press, push-ups with load.
- No deadlift platform: trap-bar deadlift, Romanian deadlift, block pull, good morning.
- No Olympic barbell skill work: power variations, hang variations, pulls, front squats.
- No strongman implements: trap-bar carry, sandbag carry, farmer handles, keg or medley-style carries.
- No bodybuilding machine: cable, dumbbell, or stable free-weight version with the same line of pull.

## 4. Method Bias
Use the method that fits the problem.

- Powerlifting: main lift specificity, weak-point work, repeatable progress.
- Strongman: event practice, implements, carries, loading, medleys.
- Weightlifting: positions, timing, pulls, receiving strength, frequency.
- Bodybuilding: targeted volume, exercise order, fatigue management, joint-friendly execution.

## 5. Contest Prep
- Keep specificity high and novelty low.
- Reduce accessory volume as the event approaches.
- Rehearse the exact competitive skill: attempts, medleys, platform lifts, or posing demand.
- Leave enough recovery to keep speed, confidence, and technique high.

## 6. Good Answer Pattern
- State the recommendation first.
- Provide one fallback if equipment or tolerance is limited.
- Say why it matches the athlete's limiter.
- End with the next decision point if information is still missing.
