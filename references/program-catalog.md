# Program Catalog

This is a curated list of external programs and program families worth studying when the user wants the skill to lean on proven examples.

These are reference points, not full local clones. The goal is to understand how strong, credible programs are structured, then adapt the useful parts to the athlete in front of us.

## Strongman

- Joey Szatmary, `12 Week Strongman Program`
  - A 12-week strength + strongman program that can be customized to the athlete's goals or upcoming competitions.
  - Includes a singles block for max-effort technique work and is explicitly RPE-based.
  - Best when you want event-specific strongman work with a clear peak.
  - Source: https://szat-strength.myshopify.com/products/strongman-training-template-2019
- Barbell Medicine, `Strongman Template`
  - A 15-week strongman training program focused on strength, conditioning, and work capacity.
  - Includes event-style training and flexible exercise options.
  - Best when you want an accessible strongman template with a more general strength-and-conditioning bias.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/strongman-template/

## Powerlifting

- Stronger by Science, `Program Bundle`
  - Six full 21-week programs designed to adapt to schedule, preferences, and rate of progress.
  - Autoregulated and easy to scale across lifter experience levels.
  - Best when you want a flexible, evidence-based powerlifting base to learn from.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/program-bundle/
- Stronger by Science, `28 One-Lift Programs`
  - A free resource with one-lift routines for squat, bench, and deadlift.
  - Useful for comparing frequency, skill level, and schedule choices across a large program library.
  - Best when you want many low-friction options for a single lift focus.
  - Source: https://www.strongerbyscience.com/newsletter-archived/
- Barbell Medicine, `Powerlifting II`
  - A 13-week strength and conditioning program included in the Strength Bundle.
  - Suited to lifters who want structured powerlifting work with enough volume to keep building while staying specific.
  - Best when you want a more formal strength block with a clear progression toward testing.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/powerlifting-ii-template/

## Also Worth Studying

- Barbell Medicine, `Peaking Template`
  - A short peaking block useful for meet prep logic and taper structure.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/peaking-template-3/
- Barbell Medicine, `Strength II`
  - A maximal-strength-focused template centered on the squat, bench press, deadlift, and overhead press.
  - Useful when you want a broader strength template that still behaves like a powerlifting-adjacent block.
  - Source: https://www.barbellmedicine.com/shop/training-templates/strength/strength-ii-template/
