# LiftVault Template Library

Use these as starting points, not scripts. Blend the structure to the athlete's sport, recovery budget, session length, and target date.

## Archetypes

### Sheiko
- High-volume powerlifting template with many variations.
- Best when you need a lot of submaximal practice and technique exposure.
- Good source for work-capacity blocks and lifting frequency.
- Steal: frequent main-lift practice, moderate loading, and lots of repeatable volume.

### Smolov
- Aggressive squat-focused peaking template with clearly separated phases.
- Best when the goal is a hard squat peak and the athlete can tolerate a lot of stress.
- Steal: tight phase structure, high specificity, and intensity progression into a test.
- Use sparingly because recovery cost is high.

### Candito 6 Week
- Short powerlifting peaking template.
- Best when you need a concise block that moves from conditioning and hypertrophy toward heavy work and testing.
- Steal: brief blocks, linear load increases, and a clear runway into peak intensity.

### Texas Method
- Three-day weekly structure built around volume day, recovery day, and intensity day.
- Best for intermediate lifters who still respond well to weekly PRs.
- Steal: one high-volume day, one lighter recovery day, and one performance day.

### 5/3/1
- Flexible long-term template built around a conservative training max and repeating cycles.
- Best when you want sustainability, flexibility, and gradual progress.
- Steal: training max setup, simple top-set progression, and variant selection based on the goal.

### Madcow 5x5
- Weekly linear progression template with lots of rows and 5x5 work.
- Best for late novices and early intermediates, especially in the off-season.
- Steal: simple weekly progression, 5-rep emphasis, and assistance that supports the main lifts.

### Juggernaut Method
- Four-wave block template with submaximal volume, accumulation, intensification, realization, and deload.
- Best for intermediate lifters who need more structure and work capacity.
- Steal: training max based loading, wave structure, and a built-in AMRAP checkpoint.

### Korte 3x3
- Two-phase powerlifting template with a high-volume phase followed by a heavier phase.
- Best when you want a compact block that trains squat, bench, and deadlift frequently.
- Steal: clear volume-to-intensity progression and balanced SBD exposure.

### Coan / Phillipi
- Deadlift-specific peaking template with one deadlift day per week.
- Best for dedicated deadlift focus.
- Steal: one heavy top set, speed work, and targeted deadlift accessory work.

### Mag Ort
- Deadlift-only peaking and work-capacity template.
- Best when the deadlift is the priority and the athlete can handle a brutal block.
- Steal: progressive deadlift-specific overload and a narrow peaking runway.

## Blend Ideas

- Use Sheiko or Juggernaut when the athlete needs more volume and practice before a peak.
- Use Texas Method, Madcow, or 5/3/1 when the athlete needs a simpler week-to-week structure.
- Use Candito or Smolov-style structure when the goal is a more aggressive peak.
- Use Korte when you want balanced squat, bench, and deadlift frequency in a compact block.
- Use Coan / Phillipi or Mag Ort when the deadlift needs a dedicated block.
- For strongman, borrow the structure, not the exact lift selection: use these templates to manage volume, intensity, and peaking while keeping the event implement or closest simulation as the main work.

## Selection Rules

- If the athlete needs work capacity, prefer Sheiko, Juggernaut, or Madcow-style volume.
- If the athlete needs a meet peak, prefer Candito, Smolov, or Coan / Phillipi style intensity progression.
- If the athlete needs a long sustainable block, prefer 5/3/1 or Juggernaut.
- If the athlete is limited by time, prefer a short peaking template and trim accessories.
- If the athlete is limited by recovery, reduce volume first, then keep the main lift pattern.
